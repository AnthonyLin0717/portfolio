About Me

About Me I am a sophomore studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell University, with a strong focus on hardware systems, embedded development, and real-world integration. Expand

My interests lie mainly with electronics, where I enjoy designing systems that move, sense, and interact with their environment. My passion for electronics comes from my own computer business that I started in high school as a way to satisfy my curiosity about how systems work.

I am drawn to projects that require going from theory to physical systems. Whether that involves PCB design, sensor fusion, or debugging electromechanical assemblies, I enjoy working hands-on and iterating until the system behaves reliably in the real world.

Cornell Cup Robotics – Electrical Subteam Member I work across multiple autonomous and semi-autonomous robotics platforms, focusing on electronics, sensing, and integration. Expand

For the AR Kart project, I contributed to the design and fabrication of motor control and sensor PCBs for a racing kart platform with Ackermann steering. My work focused on IMU and distance sensor integration, Kalman filtering for pose estimation, and stable real-time communication.

On the XRP Xplorer submersible project, I led sensor selection and system integration for underwater operation, evaluating pressure, temperature, and water sampling sensors while accounting for waterproofing, signal integrity, and calibration.

I also contributed to the ISS MicroMimic project through servo-driven solar array actuation, internal wiring through rotating truss structures, and supporting a 30 Hz telemetry pipeline into the XRP control system.

Cornell University – Lab and Teaching Assistant I support students in lecture and lab environments covering nanoscience, MEMS, and advanced instrumentation. Expand

I assist with laboratory instruction and hands-on training involving STM, AFM, and SEM for nanoscale imaging and analysis. I also work with faculty to troubleshoot experimental setups and improve lab efficiency and learning outcomes.

This role strengthened my ability to explain complex concepts clearly, debug under time pressure, and mentor students with diverse technical backgrounds.

Herbert F. Johnson Museum of Art – Museum Intern I supported public programs, guided tours, and curatorial work in a museum setting. Expand

I assisted with hands-on workshops, visitor engagement, research, object labeling, and collections management.

This experience strengthened my communication skills and reinforced the importance of clearly presenting complex ideas to diverse audiences.

Interests and Involvement Outside academics, I stay active through running and outdoor activities and maintain strong involvement in technical communities. Expand

I enjoy endurance training as a way to build discipline and consistency, and I am currently training for the Buffalo Marathon in May 2026.

My long-standing interest in computers led me to serve as a Technology Advisor for the Cornell Computer Reuse Association, where I help refurbish hardware and assist with troubleshooting and system configuration.

I am also involved with IEEE at Cornell, where I engage with peers, attend industry talks, and stay connected with developments in electrical engineering.
